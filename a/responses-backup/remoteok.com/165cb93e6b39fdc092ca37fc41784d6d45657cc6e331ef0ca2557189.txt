<h1 style="text-align:left;">
<span style="font-weight: 400;">Invisible is hiring a </span><br />Remote Principal Software Engineer </h1>
<div class="markdown">We are actively recruiting for this position and are excited to have the right person join us soon.\n\nInvisible Technologies is on a mission to industrialize work by fine-tuning automation and human interaction, powered by our team of Partners and Agents around the world.  We’ve been a globalized, distributed team, since before the pandemic, and we pride ourselves on transparent, effective communication to collaborate and take ownership as needed. \n\nWhat is Invisible?\n\nWebsite : http://www.inv.tech/\nBlog: https://inv.tech/blog\nOverview / Sales Deck - https://invtech.docsend.com/view/6kp3ixp\nRecorded Demo - https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL4135pGQh8yvHRwKatYrMDuKL7ODHBXHI\n\nAs a Senior Software Engineer your job is to write quality code, act with strong ownership, execute on and add to the vision of the engineering team, working with product and wider stakeholders. You will be working on developing Process Builder — our proprietary platform that orchestrates work across an automation engine and a labor marketplace.\n\nCulture\n\nEvery engineer should be constantly learning and growing. As an experienced engineer, you should be eager to learn and grow every day. We expect you to raise the bar for performance on the engineering team. \n\nOur team currently consists of 7 partner engineers and a dozen more agents and interns. The team will double in the coming year — you have an opportunity to mentor and lead.\n\nExecution\n\nThe first principle of execution is reliable delivery — you honor your commitments. You should set clear expectations for your deliverables and drive through to success. The formula for success? Say what you’re going to do, do it, and then let everyone know that it’s done.\n\nThe second principle of execution is intelligent prioritization. Your job is to put the puzzle pieces together in a sensible, expedient way. We’re not just building a product or a platform. We’re building a company. \n\nArchitecture\n\nWe are always striving to build the right thing. You are a key partner for Product, and you’re a critical stakeholder because you know what can be built and what will work. You will focus your energy on one of our core products and act as an owner.\n\nThe ideal candidate has learned from experience that technical decisions have far-reaching consequences. As an experienced professional engineer, you are always mindful to avoid technical debt and waste.\n\nPrincipals are Architects and Leaders\n\nPrincipal engineers at Invisible are able to follow multiple paths. Some of our Principal engineers are technical leads of teams and are responsible for people management of those teams. They oversee the technical vision for their area and ensure that there is proper mentorship\nOther principal engineers lead through technical initiatives. These engineers oversee broad multi team technical initiatives and own parts of our software stack (ex. Principal engineers might research and roll out new technical frameworks, might develop a new generation of our UI component library or might\n\nThe Technology\n\n- We know that if we have a DevOps team we aren’t practicing DevOps 🙂 both are listed to make it clear that we’re looking for a multi position player who’s comfortable with application engineering AND infrastructure.\n\n- A good candidate will have a strong understanding of cloud architecture including the major cloud providers (AWS, GCP, etc).\n\n- Candidates should understand underlying networking and security considerations when developing the architecture of our deployment environments.\n\n- Candidates should have a strong understanding of authentication and authorization frameworks such as IAM, Security Groups, RBAC, etc.\n\n- Candidates should have experience with Kubernetes and be able to point to deployments they have architected or managed.\n\n- Candidates should have a strong understanding of the operating model of Kubernetes and be able to explain the requirements for designing deployments for new applications.\n\n- Ideal candidates would have experience with infrastructure as code tools such as Terraform, CloudFormation, Ansible or Puppet.\n\nWe’re always eager to learn and grow and try new technologies.\n\nLocation\n\nCurrently we are looking to hire for a team that works primarily in US timezones and are seeking out engineering talent in these timezones:\n\n--  North/South America\n-- Europe\n-- Africa\n\nWorking Schedule\n\nThe candidate should be available between 10am to 3pm EDT from Monday to Friday.\n\n \n\n#Salary and compensation\n
